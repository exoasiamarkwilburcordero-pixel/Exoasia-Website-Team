The Market Entry Problem

Why Most Foreign
Entries Stall — or Fail

The Philippines is one of Southeast Asia's fastest-growing digital economies. Yet foreign companies routinely lose 6–18 months to avoidable friction.

01

Regulatory Maze

SEC, BIR, PEZA, BOI — navigating overlapping government agencies without a local guide adds months and legal exposure to any entry.

02

No Trusted Network

Business in the Philippines runs on relationships. Without warm introductions to the right decision-makers, pipeline-building stalls from day one.

03

Talent Identification

Finding senior, mission-aligned talent in a new market — without the institutional networks that took years to build locally — is extremely costly.

04

Cultural Misalignment

Filipino business culture rewards trust, face-saving, and long-term reciprocity. Transactional Western approaches consistently misfire in the early stages.

05

Slow GTM Activation

Without a tested playbook, most new entrants spend the first year learning what works — burning runway before generating any meaningful revenue.

06

No Operational Base

Physical presence signals seriousness to Philippine clients and regulators. A credible operational anchor accelerates trust and deal velocity dramatically.
